Problem Statement

Understanding the challenges in current Ayurvedic healthcare systems

Manual Diet Chart Creation

Currently, Ayurvedic hospitals rely on handwritten diet charts prescribed manually by doctors. This process is time-consuming, prone to errors, and difficult to standardize across different practitioners.

Lack of Integration

Existing software solutions focus primarily on macro- and micro-nutrient tracking but fail to align with Ayurvedic nutritional concepts like doshas, rasas (tastes), and food properties.

Inefficiency in Practice

The gap between traditional Ayurvedic principles and modern nutrition tracking creates inefficiencies, reduces accuracy, and makes it harder for practitioners to deliver holistic dietary care.

Patient Management Challenges

Tracking patient progress, dietary habits, meal frequency, bowel movements, and other critical health parameters lacks systematic digital solutions tailored for Ayurvedic practice.

The Impact

  • Reduced accuracy in dietary prescriptions
  • Inconsistent treatment approaches
  • Time-intensive consultation processes
  • Difficulty in tracking patient progress
  • Limited integration with modern healthcare systems

Learn About Ayurvedic Doshas

Discover your body constitution and understand the three fundamental energies

In Ayurveda, doshas are the three fundamental energies that govern our body and mind. Each person has a unique combination of these doshas, which determines their physical characteristics, mental tendencies, and dietary needs.

Vata

Ether + Air

Physical Traits

  • Thin frame
  • Dry skin
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Light sleep

Mental Traits

  • Creative
  • Energetic
  • Quick thinking
  • Easily distracted

Dietary Recommendations

  • Warm, moist foods
  • Regular meal times
  • Sweet, sour, salty tastes

Pitta

Fire + Water

Physical Traits

  • Medium build
  • Warm body temperature
  • Good digestion
  • Sharp features

Mental Traits

  • Focused
  • Competitive
  • Leadership qualities
  • Goal-oriented

Dietary Recommendations

  • Cool, refreshing foods
  • Sweet, bitter, astringent tastes
  • Avoid spicy foods

Kapha

Earth + Water

Physical Traits

  • Heavy build
  • Smooth skin
  • Steady energy
  • Strong immunity

Mental Traits

  • Calm
  • Patient
  • Loyal
  • Methodical

Dietary Recommendations

  • Light, warm foods
  • Pungent, bitter, astringent tastes
  • Minimize dairy

About Ayurvedic Food Analysis

Ayurveda classifies food based on six tastes (Rasa), their heating or cooling effects (Virya), and post-digestive effects (Vipaka). Understanding these properties helps determine how food affects your dosha balance and overall health.

Six Tastes (Rasa)

  • Sweet: Nourishing, calming
  • Sour: Stimulating, heating
  • Salty: Warming, grounding
  • Bitter: Cooling, detoxifying
  • Pungent: Heating, stimulating
  • Astringent: Cooling, drying

Energy Effects (Virya)

  • Heating: Increases Pitta, stimulates digestion
  • Cooling: Calms Pitta, soothes inflammation
